Chapter 2: Different Path

The eastern gate of Aramor stood open beneath the first light of dawn.
The Young Steward paused only long enough to rest his hand upon the
weathered cedar beam that countless generations of Stewards had
touched before leaving the Academy. Beyond it, the mountain road
disappeared into layers of mist and distant forests. Behind him, the
morning bells welcomed another day of formation, their familiar voices
drifting gently across the valleys.

The words he had written before sunrise seemed heavier beneath the
morning light.

There must be another path.

His thumb lingered upon the ink before he quietly closed the journal.

"No," he whispered.

He closed his eyes.

"There has to be."

For years those bells had marked every beginning and every ending of
his life. They had called him to study, to prayer, to service, and to
countless expeditions among the mountains he had come to love.
Today they marked something different.

The road ahead led toward the eastern frontier, a land few Stewards
willingly entered. Ancient records spoke of forgotten ruins hidden
beyond its mountains, remnants of an age that had existed long before
the Continental Academies were founded. Most dismissed those stories
as little more than fragments of history.

A farewell.

He did not.

Without another glance toward the Academy, he stepped beyond the
gate.

If even the smallest possibility remained that those forgotten places
held the answer he had spent years seeking, then the journey would be
worth every risk.

The road descended through ancient cedars whose branches filtered the
morning light into shifting patterns across the stone path. Birds
welcomed the new day from the forest canopy, while the cool mountain
air carried the scent of pine and fresh rain. The beauty surrounding him
only deepened the ache within.

Almost unconsciously, his fingers drifted toward his wrist.

The Steward's Band was gone.

For the first time since arriving in Zionara, he carried no symbol of
belonging except the worn leather journal fastened to his satchel. He
loosened its strap and opened to the final page.

He lifted his gaze toward the distant peaks rising beyond the horizon.

"I will find my way home."

The promise left his lips softly, almost like a prayer.

Then, carrying nothing but hope, grief, and the questions that refused to
leave him, the Young Steward continued eastward toward a land where
no map could promise a safe return.

The road ended where every map of Aramor had chosen to stop.

The worn stone markers disappeared beneath tangled roots, and the
narrow trail dissolved into a wilderness unlike any the Young Steward
had crossed before. Behind him lay the ordered forests of the
Continental Academies. Ahead stretched a land that seemed untouched
by the stewardship of generations.

It pointed east.

Even the air felt different.

Its towers had long since surrendered to time, yet enough remained to
reveal unimaginable grandeur. Broad avenues stretched between
enormous buildings whose architecture resembled nothing found in
Zionara. Bridges linked towering structures suspended above deep
ravines, while countless crystal spires rose from the ruins as though
stone and light had once been shaped together.

The familiar fragrance of cedar gave way to the scent of damp stone
and ancient earth. Towering silverwood trees twisted toward the sky,
their pale trunks entwined around enormous crystals that rose naturally
from the ground. Faint streams of light shimmered deep within them, as
though they carried memories older than the mountains themselves.
The Young Steward slowed his pace.

He had studied every surviving record Aramor possessed concerning
the Eastern Frontier. Most contained little more than fragments of
abandoned expeditions, unfinished sketches, and warnings copied from
even older manuscripts.

Beyond this place, little is known.

Now he understood why.

The forest itself seemed determined to erase every path.
Roots swallowed forgotten roads. Massive stone arches emerged
unexpectedly between the trees before disappearing once more beneath
curtains of moss. Weathered statues stood half-buried in the earth, their
faces worn smooth by centuries of wind and rain until neither race nor
purpose could be recognized.

Following its silent direction, the forest suddenly opened.
An immense city lay before him.

The city was silent.

No birds circled overhead.

No insects disturbed the stillness.

Even the wind seemed reluctant to enter.

The Young Steward stood at the edge of the forgotten city, unable to
look away.

Who had built such a place?
And why had they abandoned it?

As he took his first step onto the ancient stone roadway, a faint
vibration traveled beneath his feet.

He stopped.

He stopped before one of them.

The vibration came again.

Only its outstretched hand remained intact.

Long.

Slow.

Deep beneath the ruined city...
...something had awakened.

The vibration returned.

This time, it did not fade.

The ancient roadway beneath the Young Steward's feet trembled with a
deep, resonant pulse that rippled through every ruined tower and
shattered bridge surrounding the forgotten city. Loose stones rolled
across the pavement, while high above, flocks of pale birds burst from
broken rooftops, their frantic cries shattering the silence that had
lingered over the ruins for centuries.

The Young Steward instinctively widened his stance.

Something was wrong.

The ground lurched violently.

A deafening crack echoed across the city as one of the towering crystal 
spires split from its base. Fragments of glowing crystal rained into 
the streets below, shattering against the ancient stone with explosive
force. Almost immediately, the collapse spread outward. 
Archways crumbled. Bridges snapped apart. Massive columns 
leaned precariously, as though the city itself had begun to 
awaken from a long and troubled sleep.

He sprang forward.

A wall of stone crashed onto the roadway behind him, sealing 
the path by which he had entered. Dust swallowed the streets, 
reducing the ancient city to shifting shadows and falling debris. 
Through the haze, he caught sight of a narrow passage between 
two leaning structures and raced toward it just as another 
section of the roadway collapsed into darkness.

The passage offered little safety.

Cracks spread rapidly along the surrounding walls, and crystal shards
continued to rain from above. The Young Steward extended his
Manifestation—not to force the city into submission, but to understand
its movements. Closing his eyes for a heartbeat, he listened beneath the
chaos until the countless vibrations gradually separated into patterns.

The eastern wall would fall.

The southern arch would hold.

The tower ahead...

The Young Steward instinctively widened his stance.

...would not.

Something was wrong.

"Move!"

The ground lurched violently.

The warning escaped his lips just as he leaped clear.

spires split from its base. Fragments of glowing crystal rained into the
streets below, shattering against the ancient stone with explosive force.

Bridges snapped apart. Massive columns leaned precariously, as though
the city itself had begun to awaken from a long and troubled sleep.

The tower exploded against the street behind him, hurling him across
the broken pavement. He struck the ground hard, the breath driven from
his lungs as a cloud of dust engulfed everything around him.

by which he had entered. Dust swallowed the streets, reducing the

For a moment, the world fell silent.

Then, through the settling dust, another voice rang out.

"Stay down!"

A shimmering barrier of emerald light surged overhead as a rain of
crystal fragments crashed harmlessly against it. The Young Steward
looked up in disbelief.

Kaelen trusted him without question.

"Kaelen?"

They crossed a fractured bridge just as its center collapsed into the
darkness below. Moments later, they reached a broad circular courtyard
where the trembling finally began to subside. Dust drifted slowly
through the air, and for the first time since entering the ruins, silence
returned.

The barrier held.
Then a familiar hand reached through the dust.

"I told you," Kaelen said between strained breaths, "you'd eventually
find trouble too big to enjoy by yourself."

Despite the danger, the Young Steward couldn't help but smile.

"You weren't supposed to follow me."

"I know."

Another violent tremor shook the city.

Kaelen tightened his grip and pulled him to his feet.

"Let's survive this first."

Kaelen rested both hands on his knees, catching his breath.
"You certainly know how to choose your destinations."

The Young Steward laughed softly.

"I was going to say the same about your timing."
Kaelen grinned.

"I couldn't let you have all the stories."

The mountain remained quiet.

Together, the two Stewards turned toward the collapsing ruins as the
ancient city continued to fall around them.

The Young Steward closed his eyes, listening carefully. The violent
tremors that had shaken the city only moments before had faded into
distant echoes.

They ran as one.

"I think it's over."

Years of training at Aramor had taught them to move without
hesitation. They no longer needed commands. Kaelen raised
shimmering barriers whenever ancient structures gave way, while the
Young Steward extended his Manifestation through the trembling city,
sensing the unseen strains hidden beneath stone and crystal.

"So do I."

"This way!" the Young Steward called.

They had taken only a few steps when the ancient city answered.

Kaelen straightened and looked around the ruined courtyard.
"Let's find a way out before this place changes its mind."

A deafening roar erupted beneath the courtyard.

The ground split open without warning.

Massive fractures raced across the stone beneath their feet, spreading
outward in every direction. Above them, the towering dome groaned as
enormous crystal supports shattered almost simultaneously.

This was no aftershock.

The city itself was collapsing.

"Run!" Kaelen shouted.

The two Stewards sprinted toward a narrow archway at the edge of the
courtyard. The Young Steward was only a few strides from safety when
the stone beneath him gave way.

The ground disappeared.

He fell.

His fingers caught the broken edge of the collapsing floor, but the
weight of the crumbling stone pulled him steadily into the darkness
below.

"Kaelen!"

Without a moment's hesitation, Kaelen threw himself to the ground and
seized his friend's arm.

The Young Steward looked up just in time to see one of the immense
crystal pillars break free from the fractured ceiling above.

"Kaelen..."

Kaelen saw it too.

There was no time.

No strength.

No miracle that could save them both.

A quiet calm settled over his face.

With one final surge of his Manifestation, Kaelen strengthened his grip
and hurled the Young Steward upward with every remaining ounce of
his strength.

The Young Steward crashed onto solid ground.

"Kaelen!"

He reached back desperately.

Too late.

The crystal pillar struck the collapsing platform beneath Kaelen, and
the ancient floor vanished into the abyss with a thunderous roar.

"I've got you!"

For one terrible moment, Kaelen remained visible through the storm of
dust.

Another violent tremor shook the ruins.

He looked up.

Cracks spread beneath Kaelen's knees.

Their eyes met.

A gentle smile crossed his face.

"Don't..."

His voice was almost swallowed by the collapsing city.

"...stop seeking truth."

The darkness swallowed him.

Silence followed.

The Young Steward remained frozen at the edge of the shattered
ground, his trembling hand still reaching toward the place where his
closest friend had disappeared.

He did not scream.

He could not.

The city answered with one final, mournful groan as the last fragments
of the ancient courtyard disappeared into the depths below.

Now the silence answered every desperate hope that Kaelen might
somehow climb back from the darkness.

The Young Steward lowered his head.

"I'm sorry..."

The words barely escaped his lips.

For the first time since arriving in Zionara, tears fell freely onto the
ancient stone beneath him.

How many lives had Kaelen saved?

How many younger Stewards had found courage simply because he had
walked beside them?

And in the end...

Kaelen had given his own life for his.
The thought struck harder than the collapsing city ever could.

Kaelen was gone.

"If I had never come..."

Silence settled once more over the ruined city.

His voice faded into the emptiness.

Dust drifted slowly into the endless chasm, hiding every trace of the
place where Kaelen had fallen. The Young Steward remained kneeling
at the broken edge, his hand still outstretched, as though refusing to
believe there was nothing left to reach for.

Another deep vibration rolled through the ruins.

Only hours earlier, they had walked the same road, laughing as they
always had.

The Young Steward slowly lifted his head.
Unlike the violent tremors before, this one carried no destruction. It
pulsed gently through the ancient stones, like the distant heartbeat of
something waiting beneath the city.

Drawn by a force he could not explain, he rose and followed the faint
resonance.

The fractured streets led him toward the great circular structure at the
city's center. Though much of its outer wall had collapsed, the immense
doorway now stood open, revealing a vast chamber untouched by time.

Countless crystals bathed the darkness in soft silver light, and at the
chamber's heart rested a single stone pedestal.

Not uncontrollably.

It had become...

deeper.

He looked back toward the ruined city where Kaelen had given his life.

Upon it lay a crystal unlike any he had ever seen.

Grief still weighed upon his heart, but beneath it, another conviction
quietly took root.

Its light did not shine outward.

"If this knowledge had not been forgotten..."

It seemed to breathe.

He closed his eyes.

The moment his hand touched its surface, countless images flooded his
mind.

"...Kaelen would still be alive."

He saw civilizations rising long before Zionara's kingdoms.
He saw Gifts not merely as powers, but as echoes of something far
greater—something that bound creation, humanity, and truth together.
He saw forgotten knowledge preserved across the ages, possibilities no
Academy had ever taught, and questions no Steward had ever dared to
ask.

When the visions finally faded, he fell to one knee, struggling to steady
his breathing.

Nothing he had learned at Aramor had been false.
But it had not been complete.

His Manifestation stirred within him.
Not violently.

Slowly, he rose to his feet.
"I will find the truth they lost."
His voice grew steadier.
"And I will build a path where no one has to die because knowledge
was left buried."

The chamber answered only with its quiet, living light.
From that day forward, the Young Steward did not return to Aramor.
Instead, he remained among the forgotten ruins, studying the
knowledge left behind by a civilization long erased from memory.
Every fragment he could preserve was carefully copied into his journal.
Every discovery was tested with the same discipline his teachers had
instilled within him. Some proved true. Others he set aside without
hesitation. Yet with every passing season, his understanding of Gifts
deepened beyond anything he had imagined possible.

Travelers eventually found him.

At first they came out of curiosity, having heard rumors of an Earthborn 
Steward who sought forgotten wisdom beyond the eastern frontier.
Some stayed only a few days before returning home. Others remained
for months, drawn less by extraordinary power than by the questions he
encouraged them to ask.

The years passed.
Seasons became decades.
Decades became generations.

Regional leaders sought his counsel. Scholars carried copies of his
writings across the continent. Young Stewards traveled great distances,
hoping to study beneath the Earth-born teacher whose understanding
seemed to deepen with every passing year.

The forgotten ruins of the eastern frontier slowly became a city once
more. Broken halls were rebuilt, ancient knowledge was preserved, and
places of learning rose where silence had once ruled. Those who came
seeking answers returned to their homes carrying new ideas, while
many never truly left again. Families settled nearby. Villages became
towns. Towns grew into thriving cities whose foundations rested upon
the teachings of the Earth-born Steward.

He welcomed them all.

His academy flourished.

Not to replace Aramor...

but to continue the path he believed Aramor had never been given the
chance to finish.

The years became decades.

The academy became an institution.

The institution became a movement.

Long before he realized what had happened, his vision had spread 
across much of Zionara, reaching far beyond anything he had imagined 
on the day he first stepped beyond Aramor's gates carrying nothing 
but a journal and a question.

And still...

He had not found the way home.

night since he first arrived in Zionara.

from Earth. Over the centuries, he had watched the Auret slowly
